02 Boiler Room

Check every AM. Hot water re-cycle line must stay above 100°F. The sensor box is above your head on the left side of the first boiler, past the E-Stop buttons.

🔴 The E-Stop buttons are near the boiler entrance. Never activate them unless there is an emergency.
Hot Water Re-Cycle Line Procedure
  1. 01
    Enter boiler room. Walk past the E-Stop buttons — first boiler is on your left.
  2. 02
    Read the digital temperature sensor box
    Located above your head on the left side of the first boiler. Cross-reference with the analog thermometer on the pipe itself.
  3. 03
    If temp reads below 100°F — flip the reset switch
    Find the switch far up the wall above the digital readout. Flip it OFF, then back ON. Allow a few minutes to recover. If temp doesn't recover within 15 minutes, create a work order and escalate.

07 Pool Testing

Use the blue Taylor test kit stored next to the chlorine in the Engineering Office. Record all results in the pool checklist.

Target Chemical Levels
ParameterTarget RangeIf Out of Range
Free Chlorine1.0 – 3.0 ppmBelow: add bag of shock
Total Bromine3.0 – 5.0 ppmBelow: add bromine
pH7.4 – 7.6Low: soda ash · High: muriatic acid
Total Alkalinity80 – 120 ppmAdjust before correcting pH
⚠️ A level above 10 ppm can bleach out the test sample, giving a false zero. If this occurs, dilute 1:1 with tap water and multiply the result by 2.
Taylor Test Kit — Procedure
  1. 01
    Collect sample 18" below the surface
    Hold vial upside-down, then flip right-side up to fill. Avoids surface contamination.
  2. 02
    Free Chlorine: add R-0001 + R-0002
    Cap and mix. Match resulting pink color to comparator block. Read ppm.
  3. 03
    Bromine: same DPD reagents
    Use the bromine scale on comparator, OR multiply chlorine reading × 2.25.
  4. 04
    pH: add R-0014 Phenol Red (5 drops)
    Fresh sample. Cap, invert, mix. Match yellow-to-red color to pH scale. Target: 7.4–7.6.
  5. 05
    Record date, time, name, and all readings
🧪 Zero Chlorine — Shock Treatment: Add one bag of shock. Pour it as a strip along the right side of the pool near the bottom drains. Keep the bag close to the water surface to minimize airborne powder. Do not breathe dust.

08 Room PM Inspection

Preventive maintenance keeps rooms guest-ready. Minimum 30 minutes per room. Always start the inspection in BOB before entering.

🔴 CRITICAL — Start Before Entering: BOB → Room Status → [Room Number] → Engineering Inspection → START THE INSPECTION. If you forget to start before entering, the work is not documented.
Before You Enter — Setup
  • Start inspection in BOBBOB → Room Status → [Room #] → Engineering Inspection → START
  • Grab a new HVAC filterWrite today's date and the room number on the filter before entering
  • Grab the PM bagContains drill, bit, screws, paint marker, and channel-lock pliers
Full Room PM Checklist
  1. 01
    Test the TV
    Power on. Verify all HDMI inputs and basic channels are accessible.
  2. 02
    Test toilet flush & inspect tank
    Flush and confirm full bowl clearance. Open the tank — float should stop water ~1 inch below the overflow tube. Too high = running toilet; too low = weak flush.
  3. 03
    Test all lights
    Cycle every switch: bedside lamps, overhead, bathroom vanity, mirror, closet. Replace burned-out bulbs immediately.
  4. 04
    Replace HVAC filter & clean grate
    Remove old filter. Vacuum or wipe the grate. Insert the pre-labeled new filter.
  5. 05
    Inspect and repair side table if bent
    Test for wobble. If bent: (1) Place plastic bag underneath to catch chips. (2) Optionally unscrew metal bracket and manually un-bend first. (3) Drill a hole at the rear of the metal piece behind the pole. (4) Drive a screw through to reinforce the joint.
  6. 06
    Inspect walls and furniture for scratches/dents
    Use the black paint marker on scratched black furniture. Note significant wall damage in work order comments.
  7. 07
    Check shower handle tightness
    Grip each handle and test for looseness. Tighten with channel-lock pliers. Wrap jaws in cloth to avoid scratching chrome/brushed nickel.
  8. 08
    Pull and clean sink drain plug
    Lift or unscrew the pop-up stopper. Remove hair and debris. Reinstall and run water to confirm drain rate.
  9. 09
    If room has a tub — clean tub drain
    Unscrew the tub drain plug. Clean the strainer. Run water to confirm proper drainage.
  10. 10
    Test water pressure at all fixtures
    Check sink, shower, and tub (if present). Low pressure at a single fixture may be a clogged aerator. Low pressure everywhere indicates a supply issue — create a work order.
  11. 11
    Test the in-room safe
    (1) Press LOCK — observe battery indicator LEDs. (2) Enter any test passcode → LOCK → safe locks. (3) Enter same passcode → LOCK → safe unlocks. Replace 9V or 4×AA batteries if battery is low.
  12. 12
    Set thermostat & test HVAC
    Set to standby temp (68–72°F). Verify unit turns on and blows conditioned air without unusual noise.
  13. 13
    Spray TileX in the shower
    Apply a light, even spray to shower walls and grout. Do not oversaturate. Prevents mold and soap scum buildup.

09 Thermostats

Most thermostat complaints are simple fixes. The Engineering Office panel lets you control all room thermostats remotely without entering the room.

💡 The vast majority of thermostat calls are because the unit is OFF or set to "Auto" mode. Always check these two settings first before dispatching to the room.
Remote Control via Engineering Office Panel
  1. 01
    Open thermostat control panel in the Engineering Office
  2. 02
    Select the room number
  3. 03
    Set Operation to ON
    If the unit is OFF it will not run regardless of any other setting. This is the most common fix.
  4. 04
    Set the correct temperature
    Guest's requested temp or a comfortable default (68–74°F).
  5. 05
    Set mode to HEAT or COOL
    Match to the season and guest's need. "Auto" mode often causes complaints — always set explicitly.
10 Somfy Motorized Blinds

Battery-powered Somfy RTS motorized blinds. Chargers are on the power strip in the Engineering Office. Always seat the connector properly before charging.

Charging a Blind Battery
  1. 01
    Locate the charging pigtail at the motor end cap
    It will be tucked into a bracket. Pull it out gently.
  2. 02
    Connect the white charger from the office
    Join the charger to the motor pigtail. Ensure the connector seats properly. Do not force.
  3. 03
    Plug into an outlet
    LED shows red during charging. Transitions red → orange → green when fully charged (~4–5 hours). Blind remains operational while charging.
  4. 04
    Disconnect and tuck the pigtail back into the bracket
    Ensure no cords interfere with blind movement before testing.
Pairing a Blind to the Remote
  1. 01
    Wake the motor
    Press the pinhole or motor button on the end cap. Blind jogs up/down to confirm it's awake.
  2. 02
    Select the channel (multi-channel remotes only)
    Single-channel remotes: skip this step.
  3. 03
    Press UP + DOWN simultaneously for 2 seconds
    Blind jogs up/down to confirm it has been assigned. Pairing is complete.
  4. 04
    Test full travel in both directions
    If blind moves the wrong direction, hold MY button until it jogs to reverse.
Setting Travel Limits
🔴 If you begin setting limits, you must complete the full process. Partial limit programming can damage the motor.
  1. 01
    Enter limit-setting mode: Hold PROG button until blind jogs
    Green LED indicates upper limit is ready to be set.
  2. 02
    Set upper (open) limit
    Use UP to move to fully open. Press and hold MY until blind jogs — upper limit saved.
  3. 03
    Set lower (closed) limit
    Press DOWN to move to fully closed. LED blinks slowly. Press and hold MY until blind jogs.
  4. 04
    Confirm: Press and hold MY until blind jogs up and down
    Both limits are now recorded. Test full travel in both directions.
⚠️ Factory Reset (last resort): Hold PROG ~12 seconds until blind jogs twice. This erases all limits and pairings — you must re-pair and re-set limits from scratch.

🔑 Daily Requirement: The Doormakabra device config must be updated every morning. Skipping this means the device cannot accurately program doors for the current day.

Daily Config Update — Every Morning
  1. 01
    Log into the Doormakabra office computer
    Credentials on the password card in the Engineering Office.
  2. 02
    Connect the handheld device via USB
    Wait for the device to be recognized by the computer.
  3. 03
    Open the Doormakabra app
  4. 04
    Device Management → Select All Configs → Send to Device
    This pushes the current day's access rights and door schedules to the handheld programmer.
Programming a Door Lock
  1. 01
    Complete daily config update first
  2. 02
    Log into the Doormakabra handheld device
    Use device credentials from the password card.
  3. 03
    Bring the device to the door needing programming
  4. 04
    Program Lock → Room Number → NFC
    Click NEXT on the first menu before selecting NFC as the communication method.
  5. 05
    Hold device close to the lock
    NFC transfer completes automatically. Confirm success on the device screen.
